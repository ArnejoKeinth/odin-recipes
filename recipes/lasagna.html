Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish that has become popular worldwide. It is a layered pasta dish made with sheets of lasagna noodles, a rich meat sauce, creamy béchamel (white sauce), and melted cheese.

The dish is baked until the layers are cooked through and the top is golden and bubbly.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna Noodles
  • Tomato Sauce
  • Onion
  • Mozzarella Cheese
  • Ground beef
  • Garlic
  • Basil
  • Salt and Black Pepper
  • Olive oil
  • Butter
  • All-purpose flour
  • Milk

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to the temperature specified in your lasagna recipe (usually around 375°F or 190°C).
  2. Boil the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions. Add a pinch of salt and a splash of olive oil to the boiling water to prevent sticking. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet or saucepan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add finely chopped onions and minced garlic, sauté until softened.
  4. Add the ground beef (or beef/pork blend) and cook until browned. Drain excess fat.
  5. Stir in crushed tomatoes or tomato sauce, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and black pepper. Simmer the sauce for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  6. In a separate saucepan, melt butter over medium heat. S tir in an equal amount of all-purpose flour to create a roux.
  7. Gradually whisk in milk, stirring continuously to avoid lumps. Continue cooking until the sauce thickens. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. In a baking dish, start with a thin layer of meat sauce, followed by a layer of lasagna noodles.
  9. Spread a portion of béchamel sauce over the noodles, and then add a layer of the lasagna cheese blend.
  10. Repeat the layers until you reach the top of the dish, finishing with a generous layer of cheese on top.
  11. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake in the preheated oven for about 25-30 minutes.
  12. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes or until the cheese is golden and bubbly.
  13. Allow the lasagna to rest for about 10 minutes before slicing. This helps the layers set and makes it easier to serve. Then serve.
